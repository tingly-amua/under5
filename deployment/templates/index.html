Under-5 mortality ‚Äî deaths of children before reaching their fifth birthday ‚Äî remains a major public health challenge in Kenya and across sub-Saharan Africa.

In 2023, sub-Saharan Africa recorded an estimated 74 deaths per 1,000 live births (WHO, 2024), making it the region with the highest child mortality rates globally.

This project explores demographic, socioeconomic, and environmental factors that most influence under-5 mortality in Kenya, using data-driven methods.

The model supports Sustainable Development Goal 3 (SDG 3): Good Health and Well-being, particularly Target 3.2 ‚Äî to end preventable deaths of newborns and children under five by 2030.
